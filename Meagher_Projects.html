Project: MIT AppInventor Project

Project Name: GeoGuesser

Duration: September 19th - October 6th 2017

Course: Computer Science/Software Engineering

The objective of this project was to make a game using MIT AppInventor. My partner and I created a game where you would study a map of a location and answer questions that would relate to the map. As a team, each day we would assign a task to complete for the night. During the first week of the project, I was the one who was in charge of the project notebook. During the second week of the project, I would expand on our code and add more questions to our game. Throughout the process of making the game, our team ran into some obstacles. One obstacle we ran into was we did not know how to flip screens from the map to the question. We tried many different methods from shaking, to using a button, and using a timer, but we finally figured out a solution. Instead of adding anything to the game, we used the back button on the bottom bezel of the tablet. From this experience, we learned that when a code is not working the way we want, do not give up on your idea. Project: 2.3.2 Project

Project Name: West Point Bridge Desgin

Duration: March 7th - March 18th 2019

Course: Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to create cost-effective, stable bridge that spans the length of the Trinity River to allow people and trucks to cross. My partner and I created four bridge designs that would meet the constraints of the project. Some of these constraints included the cost must be under $300,000, the bridge deck elevation must be between 12 and 24 meters, and members had to be made of carbon steel, high-strength low alloy steel, or quenched and tempered steel. Everyday we would add or improve to our two bridge designs to fit these constraints while also being stable. Once we completed our four bridge designs, we decided on further improving on only one of those. Together, we would add improvements to make the most stable bridge while keeping the cost under the given amount. My job throughout the project varied. For the first week of the project, I was working on and creating two bridge designs, while my partner worked on two designs himself. From there we decided on one of my partners designs. When this happened, he mainly made modifications to the design while I would add some improvements as well. But my main job during this time, was to keep up with the documentation. If you would like to view the documentation, click this link. In the documentation, I focused on writing the description for the brainstorm sketches, the design brief, and any other task related to the documentation. From this project, I learned how to work efficiently during a limited amount of time. We only had eleven days to finish this project and each day we had to focus to not waste any time. Being given the same task as my partner in creating two bridge designs, also helped me be accountable. My partner was there to make sure I was doing my work, while I was making sure he was doing his. This project helped further my knowledge in structural engineering and skills with working as a team.

Project: PCB Design

Project Name: Electronic Piano

Duration: October 7th - October 26th 2019

Course: Digital Electronics

The objective of this project was to create my very own Printed Circuit Board from scratch. This was my very first engineering project done individually, so I had to hold myself accountable each and every day. For this project, I created an electronic keyboard by using multiple resistors, capacitors, and button switches to create different frequencies and simulate piano keys. Each day, I assigned specific goals for me to accomplish before class ended to make sure that my time was correctly managed. For the first week of the project, I had to create the circuit and display it on my breadboard. Once I finished creating a working breadboard circuit, I made a fritzing diagram for the different connections that were going to be on my PCB. Next, I had to actually create the physical board for my PCB, as I had to go through the masking and etching process. Once I completed those steps, I finally finished my board by drilling and soldering on the components of my circuit. Throughout the project, I had two major obstacles that I had to overcome. The first challenge I had to overcome was during the masking process. I had accidentally wiped off my whole circuit with acetone. Instead of worrying, I remained calm and redid the whole process, while making sure I was managing my time correctly. Another challenge I had to overcome was that I forgot to fritz holes for my battery pack. I noticed this late into my project process as I was ready to drill and solder my components. Without the battery pack, I would be unable to power my circuit. I remained calm, and I talked to my teacher to see what I should do to fix the problem. We worked out the problem by drilling holes in connections already on my board. During this project, I learned how to keep myself in check and to problem solve when things don't go my way. Project: 2.3.5 Project

Project Name: PLD Design

Duration: February 25th - March 2nd 2020

Course: Digital Electronics

The objective of this project was to create a message on a seven-segment display using a programmable logic device. This was my second engineering project done individually, so I knew how to create a work schedule and come in everyday trying to accomplish a task. For this project, I had to build individual circuits and then combine them together. From there, I would have to transfer the circuits onto my PLD and wire it on my breadboard. As I stated before, each day, I assigned specific tasks for me to accomplish before class ended to make sure that I was working efficiently and effectively. For the first week of the project, I had to brainstorm a message, "Friends Don't Lie" and create a truth table corresponding to that message. Once I finished the truth table, I had to simplify expressions for segments A-F. Next, I had to use the simplified expressions to build separate Multisim circuits for segments A-F, as I used logic gates to match the expressions. Once I completed those steps, I wired these individual circuits to their respected outputs and I transferred this into the programmable logic device. Throughout the project, I had one major obstacle that I had to overcome. The major obstacle I had to overcome was time management. I had been sick for the the week before we started this project, and I was catching up on missed assignments. So, when I finally was able to start my project I was two days behind. Instead of worrying, I remained calm and created a work schedule. This schedule helped me organize my thoughts and make sure that I was using my time wisely. I used my time management skills from my previous project and used it to make a bad situation better. Instead of rushing through the project, I made sure that I did not procrastinate and that I held myself accountable. During this project, I learned how to hold myself accountable and to step back and analyze a situation. I saw that I was two days late starting on a project, and instead of worrying and rushing, I took a step back, created a plan, and executed on that plan.
